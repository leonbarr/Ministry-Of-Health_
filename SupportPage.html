Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

  • What should I do if I can't reach emergency services?

    If you are unable to get through to emergency services, try contacting a nearby health facility or reach out to local community centers for assistance.

  • What are the signs of a stroke, and how do I respond?

    Signs of a stroke include sudden numbness or weakness in the face, arm, or leg, particularly on one side of the body, confusion, trouble speaking, and difficulty walking. If you suspect a stroke, call emergency services immediately.

  • How do I assist someone who is having a seizure?

    During a seizure, ensure the person is in a safe area where they won't injure themselves. Place a soft object, like a cushion, under their head. Do not try to hold them down or put anything in their mouth. Call emergency services immediately.

  • What should I do if someone is having an asthma attack?

    If someone is having an asthma attack, help them use their inhaler or prescribed medication. If the symptoms persist or worsen, call emergency services immediately.
